A brief history of the Final Fantasy franchise:

  • 1987 - Final Fantasy I released for the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES)
  • 1988 - Final Fantasy II released for the NES in Japan. It would later be be bundled with the original Final Fantasy in re-releases
  • 1990 - Final Fantasy III released for the NES in Japan. Was not available elsewhere until released for the Nintendo DS in 2006
  • 1991 - Final Fantasy IV (II in North America) released for the Super Nintendo Entertainment System (SNES)
  • 1992 - Final Fantasy V released for the SNES in Japan. The first game in the series to spawn a sequel: a short anime series called Final Fantasy: Legend of the Crystals
  • 1994 - Final Fantasy VI (III in North America) released for the SNES
  • 1997 - Final Fantasy VII released for the PlayStation. This would be the first game in the series to use 3D computer graphics
  • 1999 - Final Fantasy VIII released for the PlayStation. The first in the series to use realistically proportioned characters and a vocal piece for the theme music
  • 2000 - Final Fantasy IX released for the PlayStation
  • 2001 - Final Fantasy X released for the PlayStation 2 (PS2). Introduced voice acting to the series. Also the first game in the series to spawn a sub-sequel (X-2 in 2003)
  • 2002 - Final Fantasy XI released for PC, PS2, and later Xbox 360. The first massively multiplayer online role-playing game (MMORPG) in the series
  • 2006 - Final Fantasy XII released for the PS2
  • 2009 - Final Fantasy XIII released for the PlayStation 3 (PS3) and Xbox 360. Spawned two sub-sequels (XIII-2 and Lightning Returns). The first game released in Chinese and high definition as well as for two consoles at once
  • 2010 - Final Fantasy XIV released for PC. Another MMORPG that initially received heavy criticism and was re-released in 2013 as Final Fantasy XIV: A Realm Reborn. The re-release also made its way to PS3.
  • 2016 - Final Fantasy XV released for the PlayStation 4 and Xbox One. Originally slated as a XIII spin-off but has since been distanced by its developers.
  • TBA - Final Fantasy XVI was announced in September of 2020 for a PlayStation 5 release.

The Final Fantasy franchise has seen numerous spin-off series', sequels, and remakes over the years. They have been released on almost every major console and include many Android and iOS games. There have also been anime series' and films within the franchise. Most recently, in 2019, Sony Pictures Television was working on the first ever live-action adaptation of the series.
